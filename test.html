Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our neurology services, treatments, and procedures

For your first appointment, please bring the following:

  • Your health insurance card and photo ID
  • A list of all medications you're currently taking (including over-the-counter medications and supplements)
  • Copies of relevant medical records, including results from any previous neurological tests
  • A list of your symptoms and when they started
  • Any imaging studies (MRI, CT scans) on a disc if available
  • A list of questions you'd like to ask the neurologist

It's also helpful to bring a family member or friend who can help you remember important details discussed during the appointment.

Initial consultations typically last 45-60 minutes. This allows our neurologists to thoroughly review your medical history, perform a comprehensive neurological examination, and discuss potential diagnostic and treatment options.

Follow-up appointments are usually scheduled for 30 minutes, but this can vary depending on the complexity of your condition and the treatments being discussed. Some specialized tests may require additional time, and our scheduling team will advise you accordingly when making your appointment.

Our center offers a comprehensive range of advanced diagnostic tests, including:

  • Electroencephalogram (EEG): Measures electrical activity in the brain to diagnose seizures and other conditions
  • Electromyography (EMG) and Nerve Conduction Studies: Evaluate muscle and nerve function
  • 3T MRI with Neuroimaging Protocols: High-resolution brain and spine imaging
  • CT Scans: Advanced imaging for stroke and trauma evaluation
  • Lumbar Puncture: Analysis of cerebrospinal fluid
  • Transcranial Doppler: Ultrasound assessment of blood flow in brain arteries
  • Cognitive and Neuropsychological Testing: Evaluates memory, thinking, and mental function
  • Sleep Studies: Diagnoses sleep disorders related to neurological conditions

Your neurologist will determine which tests are appropriate based on your symptoms and medical history.

You should consider seeing a neurologist if you experience any of the following symptoms:

  • Severe or persistent headaches
  • Dizziness or balance problems
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Muscle weakness or coordination difficulties
  • Memory problems or confusion
  • Seizures or tremors
  • Difficulty with speech or vision
  • Chronic pain without clear cause

Many patients are referred by their primary care physician, but you can also schedule a consultation directly with our center if you're experiencing neurological symptoms. If you're unsure whether your symptoms warrant neurological evaluation, please call our nurse triage line at (555) 987-6543 for guidance.
